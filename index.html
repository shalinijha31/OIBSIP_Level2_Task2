DR. APJ AbdulKalam

Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam was an Indian scientist and politician who served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007. He was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu and passed away on 27th July 2015. Dr. Kalam was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work on the development of ballistic missile technology. He also played a key role in India's civilian space program and military missile development. Despite his achievements, Dr. Kalam remained humble and was known for his love for children. He often spoke to students and encouraged them to pursue their dreams. Dr. Kalam was awarded numerous honors, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, and the Padma Bhushan and Padma Vibhushan. Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam will always be remembered as a great scientist, a dedicated public servant, and a true inspiration to millions.
